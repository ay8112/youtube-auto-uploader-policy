# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and leaders pledge to make participation in the YouTube Auto-Uploader community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

## Our Standards

### Positive Behavior

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment include:

- **Being Respectful**: Showing empathy and kindness toward other people
- **Being Constructive**: Providing and gracefully accepting constructive feedback
- **Being Responsible**: Accepting responsibility and apologizing for mistakes, learning from the experience
- **Being Collaborative**: Focusing on what is best for the overall community
- **Being Inclusive**: Using welcoming and inclusive language
- **Being Professional**: Maintaining professional conduct in all interactions
- **Being Ethical**: Using the tools for legitimate purposes and following all policies

### Unacceptable Behavior

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- **Harassment**: Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, personal or political attacks
- **Discrimination**: Discriminatory jokes, language, or behavior based on protected characteristics
- **Invasion of Privacy**: Publishing others' private information without explicit permission
- **Sexual Content**: Unwelcome sexual attention, comments, or advances
- **Spam**: Excessive self-promotion, advertising, or repetitive content
- **Abuse**: Threatening behavior, intimidation, or stalking
- **Misinformation**: Deliberately spreading false or misleading information
- **Policy Violations**: Encouraging or assisting others in violating YouTube policies or these guidelines
- **Illegal Activities**: Promoting or engaging in illegal activities

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all community spaces, including:

- GitHub repository (issues, pull requests, discussions)
- Documentation and wiki pages
- Communication channels (Discord, Slack, etc.)
- Social media interactions related to the project
- In-person or virtual events and meetups
- Email and direct messages

It also applies when an individual is officially representing the community in public spaces.

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Community leaders and maintainers are responsible for:

- Clarifying and enforcing standards of acceptable behavior
- Taking appropriate and fair corrective action in response to behavior violations
- Removing, editing, or rejecting contributions that violate this Code of Conduct
- Communicating reasons for moderation decisions when appropriate
- Maintaining confidentiality regarding reporters of incidents

## Reporting Violations

### How to Report

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior:

1. **Immediate Safety Issues**: If you feel unsafe, contact local authorities first
2. **Community Violations**: Report to project maintainers through:
   - Opening a private issue in the repository
   - Emailing project maintainers (if contact information provided)
   - Using any designated reporting mechanism

### What to Include

When reporting, please provide:

- Your contact information (if comfortable)
- Names (usernames) of individuals involved
- Description of the incident, including specific behaviors
- When and where the incident occurred
- Any additional context or evidence
- Whether the situation is ongoing

### Confidentiality

- All reports will be handled with discretion
- Reporter identity will be kept confidential
- Details will only be shared with those necessary for investigation
- Reporters will not be retaliated against

## Enforcement Guidelines

Community leaders will follow these guidelines when determining consequences:

### 1. Correction

**Community Impact**: Use of inappropriate language or unprofessional behavior.

**Consequence**: A private, written warning providing clarity around the violation and explanation of why the behavior was inappropriate. A public apology may be requested.

### 2. Warning

**Community Impact**: A violation through a single incident or series of actions.

**Consequence**: A warning with consequences for continued behavior. No interaction with the people involved for a specified period. This includes avoiding community spaces and external channels. Violating these terms may lead to a temporary or permanent ban.

### 3. Temporary Ban

**Community Impact**: A serious violation of community standards, including sustained inappropriate behavior.

**Consequence**: A temporary ban from any sort of interaction or public communication with the community for a specified period. No public or private interaction with the people involved is allowed during this period. Violating these terms may lead to a permanent ban.

### 4. Permanent Ban

**Community Impact**: Demonstrating a pattern of violation, including sustained inappropriate behavior, harassment, or aggression toward individuals or groups.

**Consequence**: A permanent ban from any sort of public interaction within the community.

## YouTube-Specific Conduct

In addition to general community standards, users must:

### Content Standards
- Only upload content you own or have permission to use
- Do not upload content that violates YouTube's Community Guidelines
- Properly categorize and tag all content
- Do not spam or manipulate engagement metrics

### API Usage Standards
- Respect YouTube's API rate limits and quotas
- Do not attempt to circumvent technical limitations
- Report bugs responsibly through proper channels
- Do not misuse API credentials or tokens

### Ethical Standards
- Be transparent about automation when required
- Do not use the tool for deceptive purposes
- Respect other creators and their content
- Follow copyright and intellectual property laws

## Appeals Process

If you believe an enforcement action was unjust:

1. **Review**: Carefully review this Code of Conduct
2. **Reflect**: Consider whether the action was warranted
3. **Appeal**: If you still believe it was unjust, submit an appeal:
   - Explain why you believe the action was unjust
   - Provide any relevant context or information
   - Suggest an alternative resolution

4. **Review Period**: Appeals will be reviewed within [timeframe]
5. **Decision**: Final decision will be communicated with reasoning

## Community Responsibilities

All community members are expected to:

- **Read and Understand**: Familiarize yourself with this Code of Conduct
- **Follow Guidelines**: Adhere to all standards and policies
- **Report Violations**: Report inappropriate behavior when observed
- **Support Others**: Help create a welcoming environment
- **Stay Informed**: Keep up with policy updates
- **Lead by Example**: Model positive behavior for others

## Maintainer Responsibilities

Project maintainers have additional responsibilities:

- **Enforce Fairly**: Apply standards consistently and without bias
- **Communicate Clearly**: Explain decisions and actions when appropriate
- **Protect Privacy**: Maintain confidentiality of reports
- **Act Promptly**: Address violations in a timely manner
- **Seek Input**: Consult with other maintainers on complex situations
- **Document Actions**: Keep records of enforcement actions
- **Improve Continuously**: Update policies based on community feedback

## Positive Community Building

We encourage:

- **Mentorship**: Help new users learn the tools and policies
- **Knowledge Sharing**: Share best practices and solutions
- **Constructive Feedback**: Provide helpful suggestions for improvement
- **Recognition**: Acknowledge good contributions from others
- **Documentation**: Improve documentation and examples
- **Support**: Answer questions and help troubleshoot issues
- **Respect**: Value diverse perspectives and experiences

## Zero Tolerance Policies

Certain behaviors will result in immediate removal from the community:

- Threats of violence or harm
- Hate speech or extreme discrimination
- Doxxing or sharing private information
- Sexual harassment or unwanted advances
- Illegal content or activities
- Intentional security breaches
- Deliberate policy violations after warnings

## Amendments

This Code of Conduct may be amended to:

- Reflect community growth and changes
- Address new types of inappropriate behavior
- Improve clarity and enforcement
- Comply with legal requirements

Changes will be:
- Announced to the community
- Open for feedback when possible
- Effective after a reasonable notice period
